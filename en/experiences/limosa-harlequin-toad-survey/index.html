About the experience

Join the science team in the real monitoring of the Limosa harlequin toad (Atelopus limosus), one of the most threatened amphibians on Earth. Walk the transect with the biologists, log field data and watch how health-monitoring samples are taken. Genuine citizen science: your participation directly funds conservation.

What's included

  • Atelopus project biologist
  • Species introduction talk
  • Direct contribution to conservation

What to bring

  • Rubber or river shoes
  • Clothes that can get wet
  • DEET-free repellent
  • Camera

How it unfolds

  1. 1

    Meet the species

    Before heading out, the team explains why this toad matters, what threatens it and how it is monitored.

  2. 2

    The transect

    A slow walk along the stream, scanning rocks and riverside vegetation for individuals.

  3. 3

    Data & samples

    Every find is measured, photographed and logged. You learn to follow the protocol like a field assistant.

Frequently asked questions

Can I handle the toads?

No. Only the authorized team handles individuals, with gloves and a strict hygiene protocol. You will still see them up close.

Is a sighting guaranteed?

They are wild animals so there are no guarantees, but the local population is stable and odds are very high in the rainy season.
