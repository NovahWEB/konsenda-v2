Eco-lodge and nature sanctuary in Panama, led by biologists. Sleep in the San Blas mountains or by the Bocas del Toro sea.

Konsenda means a place without trails in the Ngäbe language. A wildlife sanctuary between the San Blas mountains and the Bocas del Toro sea, founded by herpetologist Dr. Abel Batista.

Who guides you

A herpetologist and a forest he knows by heart

Konsenda was born from Dr. Abel Batista, a Panamanian biologist specialized in herpetology and tropical biodiversity. He has spent over twenty years chasing the poison frogs of Panama, leads the Los Naturalistas Foundation and founded the Ectotermia Herpetario Center in Boquete.

You are not arriving at a hotel with outsourced excursions. You are arriving at the home of a naturalist and his local team, who open their forest, teach you to read it, and return part of every visit to conserving the Limosa harlequin toad, in partnership with the Smithsonian Institute (STRI).

— Dr. Abel Batista
