Who guides you

Nature, science and purpose

Konsenda was born from Dr. Abel Batista and his local team: a wildlife sanctuary showing Panama’s nature without shortcuts, with rigor and hospitality.

Who guides you

A herpetologist and a forest he knows by heart

Konsenda was born from Dr. Abel Batista, a Panamanian biologist specialized in herpetology and tropical biodiversity. He has spent over twenty years chasing the poison frogs of Panama, leads the Los Naturalistas Foundation and founded the Ectotermia Herpetario Center in Boquete.

You are not arriving at a hotel with outsourced excursions. You are arriving at the home of a naturalist and his local team, who open their forest, teach you to read it, and return part of every visit to conserving the Limosa harlequin toad, in partnership with the Smithsonian Institute (STRI).

— Dr. Abel Batista

Our commitment

What guides us

Four principles that order every decision in the sanctuary, from the field to the long table.

  • 01

    Science first

    Every experience rests on real research. What we tell you is what we have studied in the field, not on a brochure.

  • 02

    Active conservation

    We monitor threatened species —the Limosa harlequin toad among them— and reinvest part of every reservation in protecting them.

  • 03

    Community

    We build and operate alongside Ngäbe-Buglé and Afro-Caribbean communities; your visit sustains their economy.

  • 04

    Low impact

    Small groups, field protocols and cabins of local materials to protect what we bring you to see.
